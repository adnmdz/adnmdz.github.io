Reflection

What this course taught me about engineering

This reflection focuses on lessons from the design process, the qualities most important in engineers, and the strengths and growth areas carried forward into future practice.

Design Process

I learned that the design process is rigorous and not as straightforward as I once thought. Before this course, I saw engineering design as a mostly linear path from idea to prototype, focused mainly on calculations and technical design. This project showed me that engineering work involves much more: defining the problem clearly, testing assumptions, revising the scope, balancing tradeoffs, and making decisions within real safety, cost, cleanliness, and integration constraints.

Attributes of an Engineer

Based on this course, I believe some of the most important traits of an engineer are diligence, resilience, and strong communication. Engineers face many challenges, setbacks, and unexpected constraints, so the ability to stay careful in the work and bounce back when things go wrong is critical. Communication is also essential, because even a strong technical solution can fail if teammates, clients, and operators do not understand it clearly.

Role in Society

This project strengthened my understanding that engineers have a responsibility to use their skills to improve society. In this case, the system had implications for worker exposure, operational downtime, and healthcare delivery, which made that responsibility feel very concrete. I also think engineers often become leaders because they are expected to make decisions that affect safety, systems, and the people who rely on them.

Strengths and Growth

Some of my strengths in this project were project management, big-picture thinking, implementation, and helping with the marketing and communication side of the work. I was able to contribute by keeping work organized, helping present the project clearly, and supporting the team beyond only the technical tasks. At the same time, I know I can continue improving my technical skills, and I want to keep building that depth through more hands-on engineering work, testing, and practical design experience.
